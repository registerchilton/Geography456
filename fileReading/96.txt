Will of David Hall - 18 Jan 1808

    In the name of God Amen. I David Hall
of the County of Orange and State of North Carolina
being very sick weak of body, but of perfect mind and
memory, thanks be given unto God, calling unto mind
the mortality of my body, and knowing that it is appoint-
ed for all men once to die, do make and ordain this my
last will and testament, that is to say Principally and
first of all. I give and recommend my soul unto God,
that gave it, and my body I recommend to the earth,
to be buried in a decent Christian like manner, nothing
doubting but at the general resurrection I shall receive
the same again by the mighty power of God. And as
touching such worldly estate wherewith it has pleased
Almighty God to bless me with in this life, I give, demise,
and dispose of the same, in the following manner, that is to
say. ---

First. I give and bequeath to Elizabeth my dearly beloved
Wife all and singular my lands, messuages, and tenements by
her truly to be possessed, and enjoyed during her natural life
or Widow-hood, and at her death or marriage. I bequeath
the said lands and tenements to my well beloved son, Hardy
Almond. ---

Item. I give and bequeath to my beloved Son Thomas five
Shillings. ---

Item. I give and bequeath to my beloved daughter Delila
five Shillings - - -

Item. I give and bequeath to my beloved daughters, Sally,
Rebecca, Betsey, and Elsey. all and singular my personal
estate, to be equaly divided between thim -- except one Womans
Saddle which I have already given to my daughter Sally.
and one Sorel Mare which I bequeath to my dearly
beloved wife Elizabeth --- and I do hereby utterly, dis
-allow, revoke, and disannul, all and every other former
testaments, wills, legacies, ["bequeath" x-out] bequests, and executors
by me, in any wise before, named, willed and bequeathed

[page 2]
ratifying and confirming this and no other to be my last
will and testament.

                        And I do hereby make and ordain my
worthy [friend?] Abner Massey and my dearly beloved Wife Elizabeth
executor & executrix of this my last will and testament

In Witness whereof I have hereunto set my hand and
Seal the Eighteenth day of January in the Year of
our Lord Anno Domini One thousand eight hundred
and eight
                        David Hall (seal)
Signed sealed and declared       }
by the said David Hall, as his   }
last will and testament; in the  }
presence of each other, have     }
hereto subscribed our names --   }
Leonard Carlton   Jurat          }
William Shepard                  }
J Dixon                          }

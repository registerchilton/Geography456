Will of Anna Debruler - 29 Aug 1850

In the name of God Amen I Any Debruler
of the County of Orange and State of North Carolina
being weake in body but of perfect mind and memory Thanks
be to God calling to mind the mortality of my body and
knowing that it is appointed for all men once to die, Do
make and Publish This my last Will and Testament
touching Such worldly estate wherewith it hath pleased
God to bless me in this life.

I Item I give and bequeath to my sister Elizabeth Debruler
Three Hundred Dollars and one bay Mar[e] and further my
will is that If my Sister Elizabeth Debruler should die leaveing
no heirs of her own body the money if any shall Return
back to the Debruler family.

IIsc I give and bequeath to my sister Martha McCulloch one
Hundred and fifty Dollars for her benifet if Required and
if not she can Dispose of the money amonge her
R[e]lations as she thinks best in her Discretion

IIIrd I give and bequeath to my Brother Greenfield Debruler one
Hundred and fifty Dollars in money to be paid by my
Executor~~

IV and further my will is that my Executor sell my
Lands and stocks and farming utensils Household and
Kitchen furniture to the highes[t] bidder by vandue after
the Above distribution be made and the money arise
=ing from Said Sail[sic] shall be appropriated in man[n]er and
form following {Vis} ["which" x-out] I give and bequeath to
my four brothers Greenfield Debruler and Charles Debruler
Westley Debruler Harbert Debruler Equally or their Heirs
Representing them; I also do constitute appoint and
ordain my Sister Martha McCulloch my lawful Executor[sic]
of this my last will and Testament And I do hereby
Revoke and disannul all former wills and Testaments
Ratifying and confirming this and no other to be my
last will and Testament; In Testimony whereof
I have hereunto set my hand and affixed my seal

[page 2]
This 29th day of August AD 1850
Signed Sealed and delivered in
presence of us

Witness

Sanders Riley   }              Anna Debruler (seal)
Farmer Smith    } Proven by
                  Both

[Will Book Volume F, page 474]
In the name of God Amen, I Any[sic] Debruler
of the County of Orange and State of North Carolina being
weak in body but of perfect mind & memory thanks
be to God calling to mind the mortality of my body and
knowing that it is appointed for all men once to die, do
make & publish this my last Will & Testament touching such
worldly estate wherewith it hath pleased God to bless me in this
life

I. Item I give & bequeath to my sister Elizabeth Debruler Three hundred
dollars and one bay mare, & further my will is that if my
sister Elizabeth Debruler should die leaving no heirs of her own
body the money if any shall return back to the Debruler
family.

II. Item I give & bequeath to my sister Martha McCulloch one
hundred & fifty dollars for her benefit if required &
if not she can dispose of the money among her relations
as she thinks best in her discretion

III. Item I give & bequeath to my brother Greenfield Debruler one
hundred and fifty Dollars in money to be paid by my
Executor

IV Item And further my will is that my Executor sell my lands
& stock & farming utensils, Household & Kitchen furniture
to the highest bidder by Vendue after the above distribution
be made & the money arising from said sale
Shall be appropriated in manner & form following (Viz)
I give and bequeath to my four brothers Greenfield
Debruler and Charles Debruler, Westley Debruler Harbert
Debruler Equally or their heirs representing them,
I also do Constitute &[sic] appoint and ordain my sister
Martha McCulloch my lawful Executor[sic] of this my
last will & Testament And I do hereby revoke & disannul
all former wills & testaments ratifying & confirming
this and no other to be my last will & testament

    In testimony whereof I have hereunto set my hand
& affixed my seal this 29th day of August AD 1850

Signed Sealed & delivered in presence
of us Witnesses
    Sanders Riley               Anna Debruler (seal)
     Farmer Smith

[Will Book Volume F, page 475]
            Orange County Court November Term 1850
The execution of the foregoing last will & testament
of Anna Debruler decd. was duly proved in open
Court on the oaths of Sanders Riley & Farmer Smith
the subscribing witnesses thereto & ordered to be recorded

At the same time Martha McCullock the Executrix
therein named appeared in open Court & qualified
as such accordingly
                    Test

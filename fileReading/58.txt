Will of John Thomas - 10 April 1818

In the name of God Amen I John Thomas
of the County of Orange State of North Carolina being
of Sound and perfect mind and memory
(blessed be God) do this Tenth day of April
in the year of our Load Eighteen Hundred
and Eighteen make and published this my
last will and Testament in manner following
that is to say

First after paying all my Just debts - I give
and bequeath unto my beloved wife Elizabeth
all the remainding[sic] part of my Estate ["which" x-out]
to enjoy and dispose of as she thinks best

And I hereby make and ordain my worthy
friend James Thomas Executor of this my
last will and testament In witness where
=of I the said John Thomas have to this my
last will and Testament set my hand and
seal the day and year above writtin[sic]

Signed, Sealed, published, declaerd[sic],
by the said John Thomas the testator
as his last will and Testament
In the presints[sic] of us who were
present at the time of
Signing and sealling there of

Alexander Morphis  } Jurat
                   }  }        John Thomas (seal)
Micajah Thomas     }  }
                     Exr qualified

[Will Book Volume D, page 550]
In the name of God Amen. I John Thomas of the County of
Orange, State of North Carolina being of Sound and perfect mind and
memory blessed be God do this Tenth day of April in the year of
our Load Eighteen Hundred and Eighteen make & published this
my last Will & Testament in manner following that is to say --

First after paying all my just Debts - I give & bequeath unto my
beloved wife Elizabeth all the remaining part of my Estate to
enjoy and dispose of as she thinks best. And I hereby invoke [sic orig: make]
and ordain my worthy friend James Thomas Executor of this my
last Will & testament In witness whereof I the said John Thomas
have to this my last Will and Testament set my hand & seal the day
and year above written

Signed, Sealed, published, declared by the    John Thomas (seal)
Said John Thomas the testator as his last
Will & testament In the presence of us who
were present at the time of signing and
sealing thereof
    Alexander Morphis
    Micajah Thomas

                        Orange County November Term 1818
The Execution of the foregoing last Will & Testament of John Thomas
was duly proved in open Court by the Oath of Alexander Morphis
a subscribing Witness thereto and ordered to be recorded. At the
same time James Thomas the Executor therein named was duly
qualified.
                          Test

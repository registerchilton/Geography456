Will of Priscilla Duncan - 16 May 1829

In the name of God Amen. I Prescilla Duncan of
Orange County and State of North Carolina, being
of sound and perfect mind and memory (blessed
be God) do, this Sixteenth day of May in the year of
our Lord One=thousand, Eight=hundred and Twenty=
=nine, make and publish this my last Will and testa=
=ment in the following manner, that is, to say,
    First, I give and bequeath to my daughter
Lucy Duncan my a cupboard with its contents and my
chairs over and above an equal share with my other
children, to whom I will and bequeath all my other
property, (or the value thereof after it is sold,) to be
equally divided between them, with the exception
of Elizabeth White,- To my daughter Elizabeth I
will and bequeath one Dollar only -
    I will and bequeath my bed to my Son
Charles N Duncan, which is to go at valuation
in his part with the furniture thereof.-
    And I hereby make and ordain my worthy
Friend James Forrest, executor of this my last will
and testament. In witness whereof I the said Priscilla
Duncan have to this my last will and testament
set my hand and seal, the day and year above written
                                   her
                         Prescella (+) Duncan (seal)
                                   mark

Signed, sealed published and declared by the said
Prescella Duncan testator as her last will and testam
=ent, in the presence of us who were present at the time
of signing and sealing thereof
                            Wm. P. Forrest

       Exr. qualified       A. Forrest Jurat

                Orange County February Term 1831
The Execution of the foregoing last Will and
Testament of Presilla Duncan was duly proved
in open Court by the Oath of A Forest one of
the Subscribing Witnesses thereto and ordered to be recorded
At the Same time James Forest the Executor therein
named appeared in open Court and qualified accordingly

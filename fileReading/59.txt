Will of Thomas Lynch - 18 Apr 1815

[Will Book Volume D, page 428]
In the Name of God Amen, I Thomas Lynch of the
State of North Carolina and County of Orange Make & Ordain
this my last Will and Testament in manner & form as followeth
Imprimis. I give three hundred acres of Land to my Brother
Jesse Lynch it being the balance of six hundred & forty acres
survey - the above three hundred acres adjoining the lands of Willm.
Paisley, James Forrest & others Imp. 2 [See Note 1] I give all the balance
of my land with the Appertainances thereof to my brother
Moses Lynch Also I give my negro Man Jim with my Stock
of all kinds with he balance of all my property ["of all kinds" x-out]

[Will Book Volume D, page 429]
to my bro, Moses - That the 2nd Imprimus[sic] of this my last Will and Testament
may be property understood I wish my bro. Moses Lynch to inherit all my prop
=erty except the three hundred acres of land mentioned in Imp. 1 given to my
bro. Jesse I appoint my bro. Moses Lynch to executor this may last Will & Testament
this 18th April 1815   Sighned ~~~~
                                                  Thos. Lynch (seal)
Thomas Reeves
                                 Orange County August Term 1815
The Execution of the foregoing last Will and Testament of Thomas Lynch
decd. was duly proved in open Court by the oath of Thomas Reeve a Sub
scribing Witness thereto and ordered to be recorded

                             Test

Additional Comments:
Will Book Volume D, pages 428-429
Recorded Aug 1815

Note 1: Imprimis, meaning "in the first place" is used in an unusual manner here, with "Imp. 2"

No loose will found.

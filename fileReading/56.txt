Will of Margaret Riggs - 18 May 1828

In the name of God Amen I Margaret Riggs
of the County of Orange and State of North Carolina
being of Sound and perfect mind and memory blessed
bea God this Eighteenth Day of May in the year of
our Lord one thousand Eight Hundred and Twenty Eight
make and publish this my last will and Testament in
manner following (that is to Say) First I Bequeath
all my Just debts to Be paid for that purpose do give my Execut
ors Herein after named full and ample power to Sell so much and
such part of my personal Estate and in Such manner as
they may Believe most Expedient to Enable them to Discharge
my Said Debts --------

Secondly I give and Bequeath to my Sister Mary Newton
Fifty Sents Thirdly I give and Bequeath to my sister Jane
Akens Fifty Cents --------

Fourthly I give and Bequeath to my Sister Sarah Montgomery
Fifty Cents -------- Fifthly I give and Bequeath to my
Brother William Riggs Fifty Cents --------

Sixth I give and Bequeath to my Brother James Riggs Sen
Fifty Sents -------- Seventh I give and Bequeath to my
Sister Ann Kell Fifty Cents -------- Eighthly I give
and Bequeath to my sister Nancy Jackson Fifty
Cents -------- Nin[e]thly I give and Bequeath to my
Sister Susanna Watson Fifty Cents -------- Tenth
I give and Bequeath to my Sister Elizabeth Hall
Fifty Cents -------- Eleventh I give and Bequeath to my
Sister Rachel Nickols Fifty Cents --------

Twelfth I give and Bequeath to James Riggs (son of
Ann Kell) my part of Two Hundred acres of an
undevided Tract of Land and all my Stock of Horses
Cows Hogs and Sheep also my Desk Beds and furniture
Farming tools and all movable property

[page 2]
Lastly I Do Hereby nominate and appoint James Rigs
(Son of Ann Kell) of the County of Orang and State
of North Carolina Executors[sic] to this my last will and
Testament In witness where of I have here unto set my
Hand and Seal the day & year first above written

Signed Sealed published and                  her
Delivered by the said Marget        Margaret (X) Riggs (seal)
Rigs the Testatrix as her Last will          mark
and Testament in the presence of
us present at the signing and
Sealing There of
Thos. Wilson

                Orange County August Term 1849
The execution of the foregoing last will & Testa=
ment of Margaret Riggs deceased was duly proved
in open Court by the oath of Josiah Turner
Sr. one of the subscribing witnesses thereto &
ordered to be recorded

    At the same time James Riggs the Exr.
named in said will appeared in open
Court and qualified accordingly.

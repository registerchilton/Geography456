Will of Martin Murphey - 26 Sep 1848

I Martin Murphey[sic] of the County of Orange and State of
North Carolina being of sound mind and memory but
considering the uncertainty of my earthly existence do
make and declare this my last will and testament
in manner and form following that is to say

First That my executor (Herein after named) shall provide
for my Body a decent Burial Suitable to the wishes of my
relations and friends and pay all funeral expenses together
with my Just debts, Howsoever and to whomsoever owing
Out of the monies that may first come into his hands as
a part or parcel of my estate

Item I give and devise to my Beloved wife Polly Murphy[sic]
the tract of land whereon I now live so as to include
all improvements and appertainances[sic] to have and to
Hold to her the said Polly Murphy for and during the term
of her natural life in satisfaction for and in lieu of her
dower ["of" x-out] and thirds of & in all my real estate & at
her decease to be disposed of as is herein after directed
also all my stock of all kinds; Household and Kitchen
furniture farming tools shop tools all the crop on hand
inculding[sic] all growing crops of all sort together with
all my personal estate to be hers during her natural life
& then to be disposed of as I shall hereinafter direct

Item After the death of my wife Polly Murphy and the
Payment of all funeral expenses and her Just debts
my will and disire[sic] is that all the property both real
and personal as is above willed and disposed of be
given and is hereby bequeathed to Tempy Barton
to have and to hold to her and her heirs in fee simple
forever.

Item And whereas the said Tempy Baron is a minor

[page 2]
Therefore my will and desire is that my friend Henry
Whitted is hereby Constituted & appointed Guardian of her
the said Tempy Barton to have and to hold the custody and
Guardianship of her estate until she shall arrive at the full
age of twenty one Years

And lastly I do hereby appoint my trusty friend Henry
Whitted my lawful executor to all intents and purposes
to execute this my last will and testament according
to the true intent and meaning of the same and every
part and every clause thereof - hereby revoking and declar
ing utterly void all other wills and testaments by me
Heretofore made.

In witness whereof I, the said Martin Murphey[sic] do hereunto
set my hand and Seal this 26 day of September A.D.
1848

Signed sealed and delivered               Martin Murphey (seal)
in presence of us
Geo Laws  }
Jno Laws  } Jurat
                              Exr qualified

                       Orange County May Term 1849
    The execution of the foregoing last will & testament
of Martin Murphey decd was duly proved in open Court
by the Oaths of George Laws & John Laws the subscribing
witnesses thereto & ordered to be recorded .  At the
same time Henry Whitted the Exr therein named
appeared & qualified accordingly ---  ----  ---   ---
                   Test

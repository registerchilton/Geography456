Will of Ann Grimes - 27 Mar 1846

I Ann Grimes of the County of Orange and State of
North Carolina being of sound mind and Memory, but considering
the uncertainty of my Earthly existence, do make and de
clare this my Last will and Testament, in manner and
form following that is to say: First that my Executor
(hereinafter named) shall provide for my Body a decent
Burial and pay all funeral Expenses, together with my
Just debts, out of the moneys that may First come into
his hands, as a part or parcel of my Estate, Item.
I give and Devise Ann Montgomery Daughter of William
Montgomery (Deceased) one Bed and Furniture, to her and her
heirs Forever. (Item.) I give and devise Ann Grimes
Parker Daughter of Wm Parker one bed and Furniture
to her and her Heirs forever; (Item) I give and
devise to Phoeby Wilson Wilkerson, Daughter of Robert
Wilkerson One secretary to her and her heirs forever,

Item I give and devise to William C Sutton the
presbyterian Minister who Formerly preached at little
River meeting house; Forty Dollars for the use and
Benefit of the presbyterian missionary Society

Item ["I give and devise" x-out] my will and desire
is that all the Residure[sic] of my Estate (if any) after
taking out the devises and Legacies above mentioned
shall be sold, and the debts owing to me collected
and if there should be any surplus over and above
the payment of debts, Expenses, and Legacies that such
surplus shall be Equally divided and paid over to
my three Neighphews[Nephews] Hamilton Montgomery James
Montgomery & Hugh Montgomery in Equal proportions
share and share alike to them and their Heirs & assigns
absolutely, forever. and I do hereby Constitute and
appoint my Friend Henry Whitted my lawful
Executor, to all intents and purposes, to Execute
this my Last will and Testament, according
to the true intent and meaning of the same.

[page 2]
And Every part and clause thereof - herby[sic] revo
king and declairing utterly void, all other wills and
Testaments by me heretofore made, in witness whereof,
I the said Ann Grimes do hereunto set my hand
                                               her
and seal this 27th day of March A.D. 1846  Ann (X) Grimes
                                               mark
Sighed[sic], sealed, published and declaired by the said
Ann Grimes to be her Last will and Testament in
presence of us who, at her request, in her presence
and in the presence of Each other, do subscribe our
Names, as witnesses thereto
        Interlined before sighned in the
        words One Secretary

Robt. Whitted
H. J. Whitted jurat

Will of Thomas Cate - 5 Nov 1765

[Will Book Volume A, page 66]
In the name of God Amen, the 5th Day of November
in the Year of our Lord 1765 I Robert Cate of Orange ~~~
County & Province of North Carolina being very Sickly ~~
& Weak in Body but of Perfect mind & Memory thanks be
unto God, therefore Calling to mind the mortallity of my
Body & Knowing that it is Appoint for all men once to ~~
Dye, do make & ordain this my last will & Testament that
is to say Principally & first of all I recommend my Sole
into the hands of God that gave it & for my Body I recommend
it unto my Earth to be Buried in a Christian like & Desent
manner that the Descretion of my Exeuctor nothing Doubting
but that at the General Resurrection I shall receive the same
again, by the might power of God & as touching such worldly
Estate wherewith it hath pleased God to Bless me in this
life I give Devise & Despose of in the following manner ~~~
& form I give to my well beloved wife Elizabeth I give & bequeath
the half of my estate after my true & honest Debts are paid ~~~

Item I give & bequeath unto my son Richard the Plantation
whereon I Know[now] live after his mothers Dessease ~~~~

Item I give & Bequeath unto my Dafter[sic] Sarah the Bed
whereon she Know Lyes Item I nominate my Gran Dafter
Ann the Dafter of my son Thomas for to have an Equal part of
my Estate with my sons & Daughters & I do nominate &
Appoint my son Thomas & Joseph to be my Executors of
my Estate according to my Desire ~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Sealed & published         }   Robert Cate Senr. (seal)
Presence of us as Rob.     }
Cates Last will & Testamt. }
        his
William (X) Smith
        mark
       her
Eliza. (X) Cate   Junr.
      mark

At an Infer. Court of Pleas &c begun & held for County
of Orange in Hillsborough on the second Tuesday in May
Present his Majesties &c The Execution of the within will
was duly proved by the Oath of Eliza. Cate one of the subscribing
Evidenders[?] thereto & ordered to be Recorded
                          Test
                               Francis Nash C.C.

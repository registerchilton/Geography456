Will of William Logan - 22 Aug 1787

In the Name of God Amen I William Logan of the County of Orange
Planter being very weak in body but of perfect mind and memory thanks
be given unto God, Calling unto mind the mortality of my body and know-
ing that it is appointed for all men once to die do make and ordain this my
last will and testament that is to Say principally and first of all I
Give and recom[m]end my Soul into the hand of Almighty God that give it and
my body I recommend to the Earth to be buried in decent Christian burial at the
discretion of my Executors nothing doubting but at the general resurrection I
shall rec[e]ive the Same again by the mighty power of God, and as Touching Such
worldly Estate wherewith it has pleased God to bless me with in this life I give
demise and dispose of the Same in the manner and form following

first I give and bequeath to my Wife Agness her bed and furniture and my
bay mare to be at hir disposal and to have Creditable mantainince
of the benifat that may arise from the land I now possess Item
I give and bequeath to my Son Georg[e] Long ten pounds which he Justly
Stands indebted to me at this time, Item I give and bequeath to my
Daughter Elizabeth a young bay horse four years old and as to my
Land that I now possess I wishe it to be Equalley divided between my
Daughter Elizabeth and my grandson William Long and as to my
movable Estate I al[l]ow it to be Equaly devided between my Wife Agnes
and my daughter Elizabeth, and I do hereby ap[p]oint and ordain ["and" x-out]
["appoint" x-out] John Hart Senr and James Hart to be my Lawfull Executors
of this my last Will and Testement and I do hereby disalow Revoke
and disanull all other legasies Wills and Testements made by me
heretofore ratifying and confirming this to be my last Will and Testement
in Wittness whereof I have hereunto Set my hand and Seal this
22d day of August 1787                          his

delivered in presance of us             William Logan (Seal)

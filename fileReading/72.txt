Will of Isabel Johnston - 4 Aug 1836

In the name of God Amen

I Isabel Johnston of Northcarolina[sic] Orange County
being weak in body, but of sound and perfect
mind and memory, blessed be Almighty God
for the same, do make and publish this my last
will and testament in manner and form following

First, I give and bequeath unto my youngest daughter
Fanny, My Grey mare, one cow, her choice, one
little wheel, one bed and furniture, and all the cupboard
ware, I Also give and bequeath unto my daughter
Jane Thompson one little wheel, The balance of
my property, Stock, household and kitchen furniture
I wish to be sold, and the proceeds of which to be
equally divided amongst all my children, severally
after all my debts and funeral expenses are paid,
I do also appoint James Johnston Sole executor of
this my last will and testament; and hereby revoking
all former wills by me made, In witness whereof,
I have hereunto set my hand and seal, the 4th day
of August in the year of our lord A.D. 1836

Signed, sealed, published      Isabel Johnston (seal)
and declared by the above
named Isabel Johnston
to be her last will and testament
in presence of us who have
hereunto subscribed our names
as witnesses in the presence of the
testator.

James Patton
                           Exr. qualified
Samuel Patton   Jurat
